EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Tale of Custard the Dragon is a poem for children written by Ogden Nash. A picture book of the 1936 poem with illustrations by Lynn M. Munsinger was published in 1995. The poem has been described as “probably his most famous poem for kids".

Concept note-2: -Summary of the Poem: It’s a lighthearted poem about a timid dragon named Custard. Custard is a pet of Belinda, a little girl who lives with her pets in a small white house. She had a cowardly dragon custard, a black kitten named ink, a grey mouse named blink, yellow dog mustard, and a black kitten named ink.

Concept note-3: -The Tale of Custard the Dragon is a poem for children written by Ogden Nash.

Concept note-4: -1. ‘The Cow’. One of Ogden Nash’s best-known poems, ‘The Cow’ is just two lines long, and although we wouldn’t call it his best, it is one of his most famous so deserves its inclusion here.
