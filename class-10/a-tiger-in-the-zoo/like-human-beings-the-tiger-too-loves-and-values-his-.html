CLASS 10

A TIGER IN THE ZOO

[SOURCES]
Like human beings, the tiger too loves and values his ____

(A) belongings

(B) family

(C) food

(D) ** freedom

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Freedom is an essential virtue valued by all. Not only humans but even the denizens of the forest value it. No one knows it better than a caged tiger in a zoo. Animals, particularly the animals of the wilds, feel free only in their natural habitats.

Concept note-2: -Answer: The poet wants to convey that like human beings, animals also like freedom. They do not want to be caged, they cannot live a miserable life. If their habitat is destroyed by human beings, these animals cannot survive.

Concept note-3: -This poem contrasts the life of a tiger when it is in its natural habitat and when it is imprisoned in a zoo. On a starry night, the poet sees a tiger in a zoo. The tiger moves slowly up and down in his cage. He is full of rage but is quiet in his helplessness.

Concept note-4: -Everyone loves freedom and does not want to live in confinement. Similarly, the tiger also longed for freedom. He was so fed up being caged that he even ignored the visitors. He took to and fro steps in the cage as if trying to while away the time.
