Where do the tigers hunt the deer?

(A) in the cage

(B) ** near the water hole

(C) near the cage

(D) all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The poet says that the tiger should be in the jungle. It is his natural habitat. In the jungle, he moves in the long grass near a water hole. He hides in shadow to hunt the deer for his food.

Concept note-2: -Ans: The reason behind the tigerâ€™s sliding through long grass is to hunt plump deer that generally pass near the water holes.

Concept note-4: -A water body on the surface of earth where animals came to drink water is known as water hole. The tiger should be lurking in order to hunt the deer which come to drink water.

Concept note-5: -Answer. Sliding through the long grass he should move stealthily to ambush his prey. He knows where he can find his kill in the wild. His favourite hunt is the plump deer which he can find near the water-hole.
