A TIGER IN THE ZOO

[SOURCES]
Name the poetic device used in the line “On pads of velvet quiet".

(A) ** metaphor

(B) assonance

(C) Oxymoron

(D) Consonance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Explanation: the poetic devices used in the line “ On pads of velvet quiet, In his quiet rage"are : metaphor.

Concept note-2: -The correct answer is ‘metaphor’. A metaphor is a figure of speech that is used to make a comparison between two things that aren’t alike but do have something in common. Here, the pads of the tiger are being compared to velvet-a type of cloth. Thus, option 2 is the most appropriate choice.

Concept note-3: -Metaphor is a common poetic device where an object in, or the subject of, a poem is described as being the same as another otherwise unrelated object. A beautiful example can be seen in the first stanza of The Highwayman by Alfred Noyes, in the line: The moon was a ghostly galleon tossed upon cloudy seas…

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is ‘Alliteration’. Alliteration is the occurrence of the same letter or sound at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words.

Concept note-5: -The following are the poetic devices used in the poem ‘Keeping quiet’: Imagery. Repetition. Alliteration.
