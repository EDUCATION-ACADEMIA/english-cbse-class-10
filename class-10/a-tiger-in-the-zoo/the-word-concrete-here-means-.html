The word ‘concrete’ here means ____

(A) Made of Plaster of Paris

(B) Made of stone and dust

(C) Made of clay

(D) ** None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The word, ‘concrete’ is an adjective which means ‘not abstract’. It also means ‘existing in a material or physical form’.

Concept note-2: -He is locked in a concrete cell. The poet uses the word concrete to emphasise the fact that it is not even a cage which is half-open so that the tiger smells and sees the outer world. Rather it is a concrete cell with brick walls that makes it a closed space.

Concept note-3: -Terrorising the village! Word Meaning. Snarling: warning sounds made by animals. Baring: uncovered. Fangs: Sharp tooth of animals.

Concept note-4: -Answer: The poet wishes for the tiger to be ‘sliding’ through the foliage as this would assist in keeping the prey unsuspecting of the predator’s sound.
