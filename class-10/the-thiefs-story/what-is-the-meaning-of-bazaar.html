What is the meaning of bazaar?

(A) mall

(B) town shop

(C) ** market

(D) village fair

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -ba·zaar bə-ˈzär. : a marketplace (as in the Middle East) containing rows of small shops. : a place where many kinds of goods are sold. 3. : a fair for the sale of articles especially to raise money for charity.

Concept note-2: -A bazaar is a market that has rows and rows of little shops selling miscellaneous stuff-like tube socks, velvet paintings, and corn on the cob. Bazaar is originally a Persian word, and means “marketplace” all over the Middle East. The word was picked up by the Italians, and spread through Europe and into English.

Concept note-3: -In areas such as the Middle East and India, a bazaar is a place where there are many small shops and stalls. Kamal was a vendor in Egypt’s open-air bazaar. A bazaar is a sale to raise money for charity.

Concept note-4: -A bazaar (Persian: ) or souk (Arabic: , romanized: sūq; also transliterated as souq) is a marketplace consisting of multiple small stalls or shops, especially in the Middle East, the Balkans, North Africa and India.

Concept note-5: -A bazaar is a place where there are many small shops and stalls, in areas such as the Middle East and India.
