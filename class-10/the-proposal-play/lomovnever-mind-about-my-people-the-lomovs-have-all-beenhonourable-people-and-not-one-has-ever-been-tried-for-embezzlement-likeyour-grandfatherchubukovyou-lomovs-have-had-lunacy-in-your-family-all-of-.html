EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lomov blamed Chubukov by saying that his grandfather was tried for land embezzlement while Chubukov blamed Lomov by saying that his grandfather was a drunkard and his younger aunt ran away with an architect.

Concept note-2: -Lomov told Natalya and Chubukov that his aunt’s grandmother gave the Meadows for the temporary and free use of Chubukov’s grandfather’s peasants. So he’s the rightful owner of the open meadows.

Concept note-3: -What is his state of mind at that time? Answer: Lomov came to Chubukov to request him to allow him to propose to his daughter Natalya. At that time he was suffering from weakness.

Concept note-4: -In the initial part of the story, Lomov is seen to have a very kind and warm opinion about the Chubukov. He said that he has known him since his childhood. He reminded Natalya that his uncle and aunt had great respect for her family.
