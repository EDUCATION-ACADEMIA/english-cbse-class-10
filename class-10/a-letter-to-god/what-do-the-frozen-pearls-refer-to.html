What do the ‘frozen pearls’ refer to?

(A) ** hailstones

(B) raindrops

(C) golden corns

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Frozen pearls refers to Hailstones.

Concept note-2: -The narrator uses the metaphor of “pearls” to describe the hailstones, for they looked as white and shiny as pearls. He calls them “frozen” because they are made up of ice.

Concept note-3: -(b) ‘The frozen pearls’ refers to hailstones.

Concept note-4: -Unexpectedly, strong winds started blowing, leading to a hailstorm. Satirically the writer refers to these pellets of frozen rain as new silver coins. The innocent boys became playful and went out in the rain to play and collect these hailstones which looked like “frozen pearls".

Concept note-5: -Answer. Answer: Lencho says that the hailstorm are like frozen pearls because they are as hard and shinny as pearls.
