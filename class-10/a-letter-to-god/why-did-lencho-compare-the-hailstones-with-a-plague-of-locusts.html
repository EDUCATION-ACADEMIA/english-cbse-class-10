Why did Lencho compare the hailstones with a plague of locusts?

(A) ** because the plague too causes destruction of crops.

(B) because the plague occurs with the hailstones.

(C) because the plague causes illness to humans.

(D) because the plague is more dangerous than rain.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: There were no crops left after the hailstorm, so he couldn’t salvage any corn. Hence the destruction caused by the hailstorm is far greater than a plague of locusts.

Concept note-2: -Lencho compares the raindrops were like new coins because the raindrops help him to grow and harvest the crops, which results in more prosperity. Hence, he compares raindrops to new coins.

Concept note-3: -Explanation: the hailstones destroyed his crop. he compares it with locusts because they would have caused equal damage to his crop as the hail.

Concept note-4: -Why did Lencho say “ A plague of locusts would have left more than this”. Answer: Lencho said this because he was extremely sad and disappointed to see his entire field of ripe corn with its flowers ruined by the hailstorm. 12.

Concept note-5: -A group of locusts is called a plague of locusts, locust cloud or a swarm of locusts.
