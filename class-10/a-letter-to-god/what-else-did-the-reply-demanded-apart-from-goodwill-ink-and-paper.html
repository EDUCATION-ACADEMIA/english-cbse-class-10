What else did the reply demanded apart from goodwill, ink and paper?

(A) lost crop

(B) ** money

(C) God’s signature

(D) new seeds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"Money is the reply demanded apart from goodwill, ink and paper"

Concept note-2: -The writer thought that he needs to write more than goodwill, ink, and paper when he opened up the letter to answer it. Explanation: This idea was given up by the postman as the writer didn’t have firm faith in God.

Concept note-3: -So, in order to preserve the writer’s trust in God, the postmaster devised a plan: he would respond to the letter. But as he opened it, it was clear that he’d need more than kindness, ink, and paper to respond.

Concept note-4: -ANS. No, Lencho did not try to find out the sender because he was very optimistic about the existence of almighty and that, his letter is actually received and replied to, by God.

Concept note-5: -Lencho received the money and the letter from god but still he was not happy was it not in his attitude to be happy with what he received.
