A LETTER TO GOD

[SOURCES]
Which statement about Lencho’s faith is true?

(A) Lencho believed in God and in humans.

(B) Lencho believed in God but not the postmaster.

(C) ** Lencho believed in God but not the humans.

(D) Lencho believed in God and in himself.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Lencho had faith in God. He had been instructed that God’s eyes see everything, even what is deep in one’s conscience. Therefore, he wrote a letter to God expressing his need for a hundred pesos so that he could sow his field again and live until the crop grew again.

Concept note-2: -Answer: It is quite true Lencho had complete faith in God but doubted humanity. Although, Lencho’s entire corn crop was damaged in the hailstorm, yet he was optimistic about receiving God’s help. He did not sulk, whine and pine; rather he wrote a letter to God for help.

Concept note-3: -Answer: lencho believed that god would not make a mistake and give in what he had asked for. he believed that the people at the post office were a bunch of crooks and had stolen 30 pesos. he had written a letter to god, telling him about his problems and his need for 100 pesos.

Concept note-4: -ANSWER :-Lencho had immense faith in God because he believed that god is the only one who will help him in his bad situation . He asked God for a little shower but instead he got a hailstorm, then too he believed God and wrote a letter to him asking for help .

Concept note-5: -Lencho’s reaction towards post office employees was not right or justified but it was just because of his innocence as he could not believe that God had done such a mistake. It were only the post office employees who had stolen money according to him.
