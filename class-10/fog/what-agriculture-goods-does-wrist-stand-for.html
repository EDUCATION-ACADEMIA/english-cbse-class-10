What agriculture goods does W.R.I.S.T. stand for?

(A) Wine, Rye, Ivy, Salt, Tea

(B) Wheat, Rice, Indigo, Silk, Tea

(C) Wheat, Rye, Ivy, Silk, Tobacco

(D) ** Wine, Rice, Indigo, Silk, Tobacco

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rice, indigo, and tobacco were more successful during the Royal period and early statehood period. A helpful mnemonic for these crops is the W.R.I.S.T. crops (wine, rice, indigo, silk, and tobacco).

Concept note-2: -Rice, indigo, and tobacco became more successful during the Royal period and early statehood. Note: In order to help students remember these agricultural products used the acronym W.R.I.S.(T). and call them the “Wrist Crops.” ( Wine, Rice, Indigo, Silk, Tobacco)

Concept note-3: -From its colonial beginnings Georgia was established to produce wine, rice, silk, and indigo for England.

Concept note-4: -Rice, Georgia’s first staple crop, was the most important commercial agricultural commodity in the Lowcountry from the middle of the eighteenth century until the early twentieth century.
