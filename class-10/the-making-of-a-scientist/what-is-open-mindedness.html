What is open mindedness?

(A) Willingness to experiment and deject the outcome

(B) ** Willingness to experiment and accept the outcome

(C) Willingness to copy others and claiming it as inspiration

(D) Willingness to stay involved in a research work for lifetime

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition. Open-mindedness is the willingness to search actively for evidence against oneâ€™s favored beliefs, plans, or goals, and to weigh such evidence fairly when it is available.

Concept note-2: -Being open-minded means welcoming new ideas, arguments, and information that you typically do not align with. Open-mindedness is a positive character quality and it enables those who use it to think critically and rationally.

Concept note-3: -willing to listen to other people and consider new ideas, suggestions, and opinions.

Concept note-4: -Open-minded people tend to ask questions rather than offer their own opinions or argue. You can practice asking more questions during almost any conversation. For example, if a coworker is talking about a hobby you have never tried, ask them for more details about how it and what they enjoy about it.
