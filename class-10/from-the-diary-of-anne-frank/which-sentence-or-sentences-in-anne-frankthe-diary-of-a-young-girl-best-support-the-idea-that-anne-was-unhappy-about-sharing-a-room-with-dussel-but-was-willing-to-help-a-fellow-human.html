CLASS 10

FROM THE DIARY OF ANNE FRANK

[SOURCES]
Which sentence or sentences in Anne Frank:The Diary of a Young Girl best support the idea that Anne was unhappy about sharing a room with Dussel, but was willing to help a fellow human?

(A) Of course he thought it was all right to share my little room.

(B) ** Quite honestly I’m not so keen that a stranger should use my things, but one must be prepared to make some sacrifices for a good cause, so I shall make my little offering with a good will.

(C) But one thing amazed me:he is very slow on the uptake. He asks everything twice over and still doesn’t seem to remember.

(D) Dussel has told us a lot about the outside world, which we have missed for so long now.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How does Anne feel about sharing a room with Mr. Dussel? She resents having to help someone who seems so ungrateful and slow to understand the situation. She is not happy about it, she is willing to give up her privacy to help a fellow human being.

Concept note-2: -What does Anne Frank tell about her family in her diary? Answer: Anne Frank tells that she has very lovely and caring family. Her parents and her elder sister love her a lot. There are about thirty people near by her whom she can call friends.

Concept note-3: -ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS ● How were Anne Frank’s Holocaust experiences shaped by her individual circumstances? How did the events of the Holocaust and World War II affect the lives of the Frank family? What choices did Anne make in writing her diary? How does she describe life inside the “Secret Annex”?
