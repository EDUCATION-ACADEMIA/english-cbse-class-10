FROM THE DIARY OF ANNE FRANK

[SOURCES]
According to the selection from Anne Frank:The Diary of a Young Girl, why did Anne’s family move to Holland in 1933?

(A) They wanted to be closer to Anne’s grandmother.

(B) Anne’s father had to move because his job changed locations.

(C) ** They wanted to escape Hitler’s government.

(D) Holland had better schools than Germany.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Anne Frank (1929-1945), a young Jewish girl, her sister, and her parents moved to the Netherlands from Germany after Adolf Hitler and the Nazis came to power there in 1933 and made life increasingly difficult for Jews.

Concept note-2: -The hatred of Jews and the poor economic situation made Anne’s parents, Otto and Edith Frank, decide to move to Amsterdam.

Concept note-3: -Expert-Verified Answer Anne did want to give a brief in the diary at first because she considered it to be her real friend. Moreover, she didn’t want to be formal with her friend, the diary. She used to call her diary Kitty and did not address it as ‘Dear Diary’.

Concept note-4: -Anne lost her German citizenship in 1941 and became stateless. As persecutions of the Jewish population increased in July 1942, they went into hiding in concealed rooms behind a bookcase in the building where Anne’s father, Otto Frank, worked.
