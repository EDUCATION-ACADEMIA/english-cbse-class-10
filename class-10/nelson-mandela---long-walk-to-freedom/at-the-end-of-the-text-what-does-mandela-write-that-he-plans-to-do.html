CLASS 10

NELSON MANDELA LONG WALK TO FREEDOM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

At the end of the text, what does Mandela write that he plans to do?

(A) He plans to rest and write more about his life.

(B) ** He plans to continue his long walk to freedom.

(C) He plans to reunite with is wife and children.

(D) He plans to lead his party and the South African government.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is the moral of Mandela’s Long Walk to Freedom? The moral of the story is that the oppressor and the oppressed both lose humanity. One through hatred the other through oppression and hence both need to be liberated from these inhumane prisons.

Concept note-2: -Nelson Mandela, a remarkable leader, led South Africa out of apartheid after a prolonged battle against the National Party. He dedicated himself to achieve equal rights, regardless of race, and pursued his goals despite the volatile and uncertain environment.

Concept note-3: -He later achieved international recognition for his leadership as president in rebuilding the country’s once segregationist society. The last chapters of the book describe his political ascension and his belief that the struggle still continued against apartheid in South Africa.

Concept note-4: -"Long Walk to Freedom” is an autobiographical work written by South African President Nelson Mandela, and first published in 1994 by Little Brown & Co. The book profiles his early life, coming of age, education and 27 years in prison.
