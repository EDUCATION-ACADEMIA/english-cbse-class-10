What social class were Mathilde and her husband in at the BEGINNING of the story?

(A) lower

(B) ** middle

(C) upper

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In The Necklace, Mathilde Loisel and her husband used to be in the lower middle class until the event where Mathilde loses the diamond necklace that she borrowed from a friend which they lied about it being in repair instead of telling the truth.

Concept note-2: -Mathilde Loisel dreams of being rich and is self-conscious about her middle-class status. She borrows Madame Forestier’s necklace to wear to a ball but loses it, then spends the next decade working to pay off the replacement, sacrificing her beauty and youth.

Concept note-3: -In “The Necklace, ” Mathilde’s lower-middle class position in society at the beginning of the story, along with her selfish and vain nature, directly cause her downfall.

Concept note-4: -Mathilde could be described as extravagant because of her evident delicacy in her mind, though she is mostly miserable and yearning, for she wishes with all of her heart to live with the women of the richest class. Mathilde Loisel was born into a poor family in one of the lowest classes.

Concept note-5: -The society is divided into four classes, which consists of the upper class, the higher middle class, the lower middle class and the lower class. People of the lower middle class and the lower class do not have many privileges in their lives compared to those of the higher class and upper middle class.
