THE NECKLACE

[SOURCES]
What part of a plot includes all of the action that happens before the climax where the action is not as exciting as the climax, but they are events that provide obstacles for the main characters

(A) exposition

(B) ** rising action

(C) climax

(D) falling action

(E) resolution

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rising action can be thought of as the part of the story “in the middle”, after the introduction of the main characters and setting and before the climax occurs in the story. This part of the story is often where the main character faces challenges and must overcome obstacles to achieve their goal.

Concept note-2: -Falling Action-resolution of character’s crisis. (The events after the climax which close the story.)

Concept note-3: -Rising Action The rising action normally begins with an inciting incident, or a moment that sets your story into action. As it progresses, you’ll have multiple moments of conflict that escalate and create tension as the story moves toward the climax.

Concept note-4: -Falling Action: The action falls immediately after the turning point. Events that occur in the falling action are the after-effects or consequences of the climax.

Concept note-5: -The rising action is an increasing line (indicating the building of tension), all the way up until it reaches the climax-the peak or turning point of the story, and when everything changes.
