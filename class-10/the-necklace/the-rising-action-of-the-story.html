The rising action of the story:

(A) the end

(B) the most tense part/turning point

(C) the beginning where we are introduced to characters, setting, and conflict

(D) ** where the conflict builds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rising action is usually commenced by an inciting incident-typically an event that sets up the main conflict and lays down obstacles for the main character(s). Rising action should build toward the climax of the story. Create several narrative subplots to intrigue your reader or viewer.

Concept note-2: -In a story, the rising action is the part where the conflict begins to build and the stakes start to get higher. This is usually when the protagonist starts to face challenges and obstacles that test their abilities. The rising action often leads to the climax, which is the storyâ€™s most exciting and suspenseful part.

Concept note-3: -Rising action in literature refers to all the events that happen in a story on the way to the climax. The rising action pushes the plot along, building tension to keep us invested in the story as it moves forward. It is the second stage in the plot, after the initial exposition.
