Which of the following is NOT part of the rising action?

(A) The husband brings home the party invitation

(B) ** Madame Loisel is described as grieving for luxuries, upset at her life as the wife of a clerk

(C) Mme. Loisel buys a new dress with her husband’s money

(D) Mme. Loisel borrows a necklace from her friend

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In “The Necklace", Madame Loisel is a “very pretty", charming, young woman. In the first paragraph of the story, she was directly described as: The girl was one of those pretty and charming young creatures who sometimes are born, as if by a slip of fate, into a family of clerks.

Concept note-2: -which word best describes madame loisel’s feeling at the end of the story? astonishment. from her thoughts, speeches, and behavior, we can conclude that madame loisel admiers a society that values about all.

Concept note-3: -The Loisels are so upset about losing the necklace because it is borrowed. Mathilde borrowed it from an old friend, Madame Forestier, because Mathilde did not own any and Madame Forestier was wealthy. The Loisels believe that the necklace is made of real diamonds and must be incredibly expensive.
