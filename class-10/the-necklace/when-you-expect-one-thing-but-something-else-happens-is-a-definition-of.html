When you expect one thing but something else happens is a definition of

(A) characterization

(B) ** irony

(C) inciting incident

(D) setting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Situational irony is when we expect one thing, but get the opposite.

Concept note-2: -Situational irony occurs when there is a difference between what is expected to happen and what actually happens. For example, a fire station burning down is a case of situational irony. Verbal irony is when a character says something that is different from what he or she really means, or how he or she really feels.

Concept note-3: -Verbal irony: The definition of verbal irony is a statement in which the speaker’s words are incongruous with the speaker’s intent. A speaker says one thing while meaning another, resulting in an ironic clash between their intended meaning and their literal meaning.

Concept note-4: -: the use of words to express something other than and especially the opposite of the literal meaning.

Concept note-5: -The three most common kinds you’ll find in literature classrooms are verbal irony, dramatic irony, and situational irony.
