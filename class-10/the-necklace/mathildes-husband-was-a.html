THE NECKLACE

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Mathilde’s husband was a

(A) trash collector

(B) ** clerk

(C) janitor

(D) teacher

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Monsieur Loisel is Mathilde’s husband and a government clerk. He procures the invitation to the ball for Mathilde and helps to work off the debt for the necklace .

Concept note-2: -Solution : Monsieur Loisel is a clerk at the Ministry of Education, having no wealth or status. He works a modest job and provides Mathilde with a modest life.

Concept note-3: -The opinion we get from Mathilde’s husband is that he is loving, caring, supporting and hardworking. We see how he gives Mathilde the four hundred francs which he had saved up for his gun so that Mathilde may have her dress. When she is in distress he tries to comfort her and do whatever is possible in his means.

Concept note-4: -In spite of her beauty and charm, Mathilde has no chance of marrying an important or wealthy man. This is because she was born into a lower-middle-class family and has no dowry. Instead, she marries Monsieur Loisel, a minor civil servant who works at the Ministry of Public Instruction.

Concept note-5: -Mathilde was born into a family of clerks. She didn’t have the means of being married into an aristocratic family. Thus, she was married to a ‘minor official’ at the Ministry of Education. Was this answer helpful?
