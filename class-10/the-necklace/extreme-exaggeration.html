extreme exaggeration

(A) personification

(B) foreshadow

(C) ** hyperbole

(D) round character

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hyperbole is a rhetorical and literary technique where an author or speaker intentionally uses exaggeration and overstatement for emphasis and effect.

Concept note-2: -Hyperbole is a figure of speech or literary device that uses deliberate and extreme exaggeration to create a strong emotional response from the reader, emphasize a statement, or add a sense of drama. For example, you might say I’m so hungry I could eat a horse. You couldn’t literally eat an entire horse.

Concept note-3: -Hyperbole is an exaggerated statement or claim that is not meant to be taken literally. For example, I’ve told you a million times!

Concept note-4: -1. I’m so hungry I could eat a horse. 2. She’s as old as the hills.
