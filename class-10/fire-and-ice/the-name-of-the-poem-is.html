The name of the poem is

(A) ** Robert Frost

(B) Shakespeare

(C) William Wordsworth

(D) John Keats

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Robert Frost’s most famous poems included “The Gift Outright, ” “Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening, ” “Birches, ” “Mending Wall, ” “The Road Not Taken, ” and “Nothing Gold Can Stay.”

Concept note-2: -’Mending Wall’. One of Frost’s most famous poems, ‘Mending Wall’ is about the human race’s primitive urge to ‘mark its territory’ and our fondness for setting clear boundaries for our houses and gardens.

Concept note-3: -Robert Frost wrote at least 100 poems, according to various sources listing his works. He also wrote several collections containing these poems, though some of these poems appear in several volumes.

Concept note-4: -Who Was Robert Frost? Robert Frost was an American poet and winner of four Pulitzer Prizes. Famous works include “Fire and Ice, ” “Mending Wall, ” “Birches, ” “Out Out, ” “Nothing Gold Can Stay” and “Home Burial.” His 1916 poem, “The Road Not Taken, ” is often read at graduation ceremonies across the United States.

Concept note-5: -Frost is still widely thought of as a nature poet, but this is a misconception. Although most of his poems are filled with nature images, his real subject is humanity. Frost admitted that he “had only three or four pure nature poems. The rest were human portraits with a nature setting."
