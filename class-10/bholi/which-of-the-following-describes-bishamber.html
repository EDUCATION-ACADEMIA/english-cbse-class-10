BHOLI

[SOURCES]
which of the following describes bishamber

(A) ** he had a several thousands in the bank

(B) ** he was as old as bholi’s father

(C) ** he also limps

(D) ** he was a well to do grosser

(E) ** he was a greedy person

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Bishamber was an old, lame and a rich man who had grown up children. He was mean, greedy and selfish. He was an opportunist who tried to take advantage of Bholi’s weaknesses by demanding five thousand rupees as dowry.

Concept note-2: -Bholi’s parents accepted Bishamber’s proposal because he had a big shop, his own house and several thousand in the bank. He did not even ask for dowry. Her mother thought that Bholi was lucky to get such a well-to-do bridegroom.

Concept note-3: -Answers. Bishamber was a middle age man. He was nearly as old as Bholi’s father. He limped while walking.

Concept note-4: -Bishamaber was a lame and rich man. He was as old as Bholi’s father. He agreed to marry Bholi without any dowry. When Bishamber saw the pock-marks on Bholi’s face, he demanded five thousand rupees from Ramlal.

Concept note-5: -Bishambhar Nath Pande (23 December 1906 – 1 June 1998) was a freedom fighter, social worker, and parliamentarian in India. Pande devoted his life to the cause of national integration, and to the spread of the Gandhian way of life.
