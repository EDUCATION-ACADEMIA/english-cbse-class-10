Why did the narrator go to New Mullion?

(A) he was fed up of city life

(B) ** to serve summons to Oliver Lutkins

(C) to witness a court case

(D) to enjoy the scenic beauty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer : The lawyer is sent to New Mullion to serve summons on Oliver Lutkins, who is needed as a witness in a law case.

Concept note-2: -He was sent to New Mullion to serve summons on Oliver Lutkins, who was needed as a witness in a law case. He was frustrated by the routine life of the city. As new Mullion was located forty miles in the country, he expected New Mullion to be a sweet and simple country village.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The lawyer is sent to New Mullion to serve summons on a man called Oliver Lutkins. Lutkins was needed as a witness in a law case and he had ignored all letters from the law firm. He thinks that New Mullion would be a sweet and simple country village.

Concept note-4: -A man who knew Lutkins went with him. On reaching New Mullion, the narrator was shocked to find that the hack driver himself was Oliver Lutkins. Lutkins and his mother laughed at him. Feeling insulted and humiliated, the narrator served summons on Lutkins.

Concept note-5: -Answer: The lawyer was sent to New Mullion to serve a summons on Oliver Lutkins, who was needed as a witness in a law case.
