Rhyme scheme in stanza one

(A) ** Aaba ccc

(B) Aafa ggg

(C) Aaha

(D) Aada eee

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lines designated with the same letter rhyme with each other. For example, the rhyme scheme ABAB means the first and third lines of a stanza, or the “A”s, rhyme with each other, and the second line rhymes with the fourth line, or the “B”s rhyme together.

Concept note-2: -Solution. The rhyme scheme of : stanza 1: abab.

Concept note-3: -A sonnet is a poem with fourteen lines that follows a strict rhyme scheme (abab cdcd efef gg) and specific structure. Each line contains ten syllables, and is written in iambic pentameter in which a pattern of a non-emphasized syllable followed by an emphasized syllable is repeated five times.

Concept note-4: -Rhythm scheme is the poet’s deliberate placement of line patterns in a poem to rhyme with other lines of a stanza or whole poem. It can be identified by noticing the end words of each line that rhyme with each other perfectly making a meaning. Here the rhythm pattern is AABA.

Concept note-5: -Chain rhyme is a rhyme scheme that links together stanzas by carrying a rhyme over from one stanza to the next. A number of verse forms use chain rhyme as an integral part of their structures. One example is terza rima, which is written in tercets with a rhyming pattern ABA BCB CDC.
