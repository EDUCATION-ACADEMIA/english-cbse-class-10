AMANDA

[SOURCES]
Name the literary device used in the line “Don’t bite your nails, Amanda! Don’t hunch your shoulders, Amanda!”

(A) ** Anaphora

(B) Enjambment

(C) Metaphor

(D) Alliteration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The literary device used in the line “Don’t bite your nails, Amanda! Don’t hunch your shoulders, Amanda!” is Anaphora.

Concept note-2: -(d) Literary device used in third line is Alliteration. ‘Stop that slouching and sit up straight’.

Concept note-3: -Explanation: Alliteration-Alliteration refers to the repetition of initial consonant sounds, typically, in two or more nearby words or syllables.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is ‘Alliteration’.
