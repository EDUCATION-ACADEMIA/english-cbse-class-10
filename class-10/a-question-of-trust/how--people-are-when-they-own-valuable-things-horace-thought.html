“How ____ people are when they own valuable things, Horace thought”

(A) selfish

(B) ** foolish

(C) lucky

(D) fortunate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How foolish people are when they own valuable things, Horace thought. A magazine article had described this house, giving a plan of all the rooms and a picture of this room. The writer had even mentioned that the painting hid a safe! But Horace found that the flowers were hindering him in his work.

Concept note-2: -A Question of Trust Horace was contempalting that people, who own valuable things tend to be foolish. The peolpe who lived in the house had let a magazine describe this house through an article that displayed a plan of all the rooms. Surprisingly, the writer had even mentioned that the painting hid a safe.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The statement “How foolish people are when they own valuable things” does not necessarily hold true for Horace Danby himself. Throughout the story “A Question of Trust”, Horace is shown to be a wise and responsible owner of his valuable possessions.

Concept note-4: -Horace said, “My first thought was to run.” “Of course, you could do that. But I would telephone the police and tell them all about you. They’d get you at once.”

Concept note-5: -Though Horace planned everything meticulously; he failed led because of the smart lady. She posed herself to be the owner of the house and convinced him to open the shelf as she had not the password of the shelf. Horace got trapped in her plot.
