Why did Horace rob a safe every year?

(A) to live in style

(B) to support his family

(C) to live comfortably

(D) ** to buy rare and valuable books

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -2. Why does he steal every year? Ans: Horace Danby was fond of collecting expensive and rare books, but he was not rich enough to fund his wish. So instead of working hard or saving money, he would rob a safe every year.

Concept note-2: -Answer: Horace robbed a safe every year so that he could buy the rare and expensive books that he was fond of collecting. Each year he planned carefully just what he would do, stole enough to last for twelve months, and secretly bought the books he loved through an agent.

Concept note-3: -Ans. Horace Danby loved rare and expensive books. He bought them secretly through an agent. But for this, he had to rob a safe every year because he did not have enough money to buy these books.

Concept note-4: -It was not difficult for Horace Danby to open the safe because he had lived with locks and safes all his life.

Concept note-5: -For two days, he kept his promise given to the kind, young lady in red. On the third day, he realised that he would have to look for another safe to buy books. But he never got that chance because the police arrested him for the jewel robbery at Shotover Grange.
