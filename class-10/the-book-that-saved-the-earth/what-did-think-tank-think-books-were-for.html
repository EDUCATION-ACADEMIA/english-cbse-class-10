What did Think Tank think books were for?

(A) hearing

(B) reading

(C) ** eating

(D) smelling

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Think-Tank first thinks that the books are sandwiches that earthlings eat. He calls the cover of the book the two slices of bread and the pages as some sort of filling.

Concept note-2: -Question 9: What does Noodle suggest to Think-Tank about the books? Noodle tells Think-Tank that the Earthlings did not listen to the sandwiches, they; opened and watched them. Think-Tank had thought that the books were sandwiches. He told hint that they use these books as a device of communication.

Concept note-3: -Ans. Think-Tank makes ridiculous and fantastic guesses about books that are found on the planet Earth. Using his unusual fertile brain, he makes a wild guess that books are actually sandwiches, the staple diet of the Earthlings. To confirm his opinion, he asks the captain Omega to eat a book.

Concept note-4: -Omega was the captain of the space probe army. He worked for great and mighty think-tank in Mars space control. He tried to eat a book because think-tank told him that the book was a sandwich and ordered him to eat it.

Concept note-5: -Noodle hesitantly informs Think-Tank that the books were a sort of communication device. He told that he had seen surveyor films of those ‘sandwiches’. He had noticed that the Earthlings did not eat them. They used them as sort of communication device.
