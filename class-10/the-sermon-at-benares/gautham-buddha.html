CLASS 10

THE SERMON AT BENARES

[SOURCES]
Gautham Buddha

(A) 564-484 B.C

(B) 565-485 B.C

(C) ** 563-483 B.C

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The founder of Buddhism, Siddhartha Gautama, was born circa 563 BCE into a wealthy family. Gautama rejected his life of riches and embraced a lifestyle of asceticism, or extreme self-discipline. After 49 consecutive days of meditation, Gautama became the Buddha, or “enlightened one”.

Concept note-2: -Complete answer: Siddharth Gautama was a prince of the Shakya clan. He was born in Lumbini in 563 BCE-483BCE.

Concept note-3: -Siddhartha Gautama, the Lord Buddha, was born in 623 B.C. in the famous gardens of Lumbini, which soon became a place of pilgrimage.

Concept note-4: -Buddhism, founded in the late 6th century B.C.E. by Siddhartha Gautama (the “Buddha"), is an important religion in most of the countries of Asia.
